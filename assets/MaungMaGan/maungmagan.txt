1. Introduction / Overview
Maungmagan Beach is one of the most beautiful coastal destinations in Myanmar, well-known for its long stretch of golden sand, fresh local seafood, and a relaxing lifestyle. The beach offers a peaceful natural environment with stunning views, fresh local seafood, and a relaxing lifestyle. Travelers love this destination because of its stunning sunset scenery, wide beachfront, and local fishing activities. Major highlights include visiting the nearby Myaw Yit Pagoda, exploring the local fishing village, and enjoying the tranquil atmosphere on weekdays.
2. History
The name “Maungmagan Beach” originates from a local legend and translates to mean “where the emperor's concubines bathe”. Historically, the area has been associated with a fishing community and is also recognized as the second oldest established beach locale in Myanmar after Ngapali. A unique tradition in this area is witnessing the local fishermen haul in their nets on the beach in the mornings, which reflects the lifestyle and beliefs of the local people. One interesting fact about the beach is that it has a very wide sandbank, so the water goes a long way out at low tide.
3. Location
The beach is located in Dawei (city) in Tanintharyi Region, Myanmar. It lies approximately 12 km (7.5 miles) away from Dawei (nearest major town). The coordinates of the beach are approximately Latitude: 14.08° N and Longitude: 98.05° E (approximate based on city location), making it easy to locate on digital maps.

4. How to Get There / Car Route
Travelers can reach the beach by car, bus, or flight, depending on their starting location.

    By car: The most common route is from Dawei city center to Maungmagan along a well-sealed road, which usually takes around 20-30 minutes. The estimated cost for toll fees and fuel is minimal as it's a short local drive.
    Bus transportation: Local buses are also available from Dawei city, with ticket prices ranging from around 1,000 MMK to 2,000 MMK, depending on the bus class and local rates (prices are estimates).
    For air travel: Visitors can fly from Yangon to Dawei Airport (DUI), and then take a car to the beach. Average flight prices are around 100,000 MMK to 200,000 MMK (prices are estimates and vary seasonally).
    Parking fees: Parking is often free in certain areas, though some private resorts may have a fee. There may not be a formal entrance fee for the public beach itself.

5. Best Time to Visit
The ideal time to visit the beach is during the dry season, especially from November to April, when the weather is sunny, waves are calm, and outdoor activities are most enjoyable. During the rainy season (May to October), strong waves and rain may limit travel activities, so visitors should plan accordingly. Average temperatures during the best months range between 25°C and 32°C with minimal rainfall and pleasant sea breeze.
6. Activities
Visitors can enjoy swimming (with caution in some areas due to undertows), snorkeling, beach sports like soccer, boat trips to nearby islands (like the Moscos Islands), and sunset photography. Snorkeling gear rental usually costs around 5,000 MMK to 10,000 MMK, while a boat tour may range from 30,000 MMK to 50,000 MMK depending on the duration and number of people. Jet-ski rides are generally not available, as it is a more local, undeveloped beach. Local guided tours are also available, offering insights into the fishing villages and nearby nature spots. Relaxing sunset views and general beach walking are free and highly recommended.
7. Local Food & Souvenirs
The beach area is famous for its fresh seafood dishes such as grilled fish, prawns, and crabs, typically priced between 5,000 MMK and 15,000 MMK per dish. Popular local restaurants and food stalls serve traditional Myanmar dishes like curries and Burmese salads. Visitors can also purchase handmade souvenirs, including seashell crafts, local textiles (like longyis), and coastal-themed clothing, usually costing 1,000 MMK to 5,000 MMK. Recommended markets near the beach include the Maungmagan fish market in the morning and the main Si Pin Tharyar Zei in Dawei city.
8. Lifestyle (Local Culture & People)
The local community consists mainly of fishing families who follow traditional fishing practices passed down through generations. Villagers are friendly and respectful toward visitors. Travelers should behave responsibly by avoiding littering, respecting restricted fishing areas, and dressing modestly (locals often swim fully clothed) when visiting local villages or religious sites. Festivals and cultural events may occur during certain seasons, showcasing local music, food, and customs.
9. Accommodation
The beach offers a range of accommodation options, from budget guesthouses to luxury beachfront hotels. Prices generally range from 40,000 MMK to 150,000 MMK per night (or approximately 25 USD to 75 USD), depending on facilities such as Wi-Fi, air-conditioning, sea view rooms, and private bathrooms. Some options, like Coconut Guest House or Maungmagan Beach Resort, are well-known.
10. Useful Tips
Visitors should bring sunscreen, a hat, beach slippers (due to potential glass/rubbish in some areas), drinking water, and enough cash, as some shops may not accept digital payments. Swimming should only be done in safe zones, and travelers should pay attention to warning flags or lifeguard instructions.
Nearby Pagodas to Visit:

    Myaw Yit Pagoda: A beautiful golden pagoda located on a small island offshore, connected to the mainland by a bridge at San Maria Beach, a short drive south.
    Shinmokhti Paya: A historic shrine dating back to 1438, located a bit further from the main beach area but a significant cultural site.
    Shwethalyaung Daw Mu: The largest reclining Buddha in the country, located near Dawei city.

In case of emergency, lifeguard services are not guaranteed (local help is available), and local police and clinics are available in Dawei city.

    Police Station: Dawei Township Police Station (contact locally for number)
